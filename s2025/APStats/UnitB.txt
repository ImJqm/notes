LLN: Law of Large Numbers, if u run infinite trials they will asymptote to the theoretical probability

How to interpret probability: If you take a very large sample of ___, about __% of them will ____

LLN does not compensate for previous outcomes, 6 tails does not mean 6 heads next

Probability:
P(A) = # of outcomes for A/ total number of outcomes

P(A^c) = 1-P(A)

Mutually exclusive: If 2 events CANNOT occur at the same time, they are mutually exclusive

P(A and B) = 0 if A and B are mutually exclusive

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) IF A and B are mutually exclusive

General Addition rule, typically if A and B are not mutually exclusive: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

If A & B are independent, the P(B) does not affect P(A) and vice versa, so P(A given B) = P(A) and vice versa

Events that occur without replacement are usually independent, indep events arenâ€™t affected by past events

Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B), Dependent Events: P(A) * P(B given A) 

Conditional Probability: P(A given B) = P(A and B)/P(B)
