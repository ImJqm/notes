Distributions:
Probability Dist for random Variable:

All probas must be between 0 and 1, Sum of probs = 1, Mean = sum of each result*its prob, sd = sqrt(sum of (x- mean)^2 * P(x))

Binomial Dist:
B: Binary(2 outcomes), 

I:Independent, each outcome is indep of the others, 

N: n(num of trials) is predetermined, 

S: Same, probabilities to not change

Mean = n*p, Sd = sqrt:(np(1-p)) where n = number of trials and p = the probability of succes

Prob of x success in n trials, nCx*p^x*(1-p)^n-x,    P(fail)= q = 1-p, P(success on 1 trial) = p,

Can be generalized to the normal dist, if the large counts condition is true, np & n(1-p) >=10

Geometric Dist:
B: Binary(2 outcomes), 

I:Independent, each outcome is indep of the others,

F:Were looking for the first success, words like until, n is not predetermined, 

S: Same, probabilities to not change

Mean = 1/p, SD: sqrt(1-p)/p, where p = prob of success

Model: 1st success on xth attempt: ((1-p)^x-1)*p, Skewed right always

Interpretation: Mean: Over many, many _ the average _ is about mean SD: The _ typically vary by about SD from the mean of MEAN

Binomial Coefficient = number of paths that lead to a certain outcome

nPr = Permutation = number of ways you can arrange n items with r choice to make = n!/(n-r)!, order matters

nCx = Combination = how many ways can you arrange x items with r choices to make where order does not matter = n!/x!(n-x)!
