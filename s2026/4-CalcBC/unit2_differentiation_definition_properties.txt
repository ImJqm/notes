AP CALCULUS BC - UNIT 2: DIFFERENTIATION: DEFINITION AND FUNDAMENTAL PROPERTIES
Exam Weighting: 4-7% BC
=================================================

AVERAGE RATE OF CHANGE
Average rate of change of f on [a, b] = [f(b) - f(a)] / (b - a)
This equals the slope of the secant line between (a, f(a)) and (b, f(b)).
Equivalent forms of the difference quotient:
  [f(a + h) - f(a)] / h
  [f(x) - f(a)] / (x - a)

DEFINITION OF THE DERIVATIVE
The instantaneous rate of change of f at x = a:
  f'(a) = lim [f(a + h) - f(a)] / h as h -> 0
  f'(a) = lim [f(x) - f(a)] / (x - a) as x -> a
Both are equivalent definitions. The limit must exist for the derivative to exist.
The derivative of f as a function: f'(x) = lim [f(x+h) - f(x)] / h as h -> 0

DERIVATIVE NOTATION
f'(x), y', dy/dx, d/dx[f(x)] are all equivalent notations.
Higher-order: f''(x), d^2y/dx^2, f^(n)(x), d^n y/dx^n

GEOMETRIC MEANING
The derivative at a point equals the slope of the tangent line at that point.
Equation of tangent line at (a, f(a)): y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)

ESTIMATING DERIVATIVES
From a table: use a difference quotient with nearby values.
From a graph: estimate the slope of the tangent line visually.
Technology can calculate or estimate derivatives.

DIFFERENTIABILITY AND CONTINUITY
If f is differentiable at a, then f is continuous at a.
Contrapositive: if f is not continuous at a, then f is not differentiable there.
A function can be continuous but not differentiable (the converse does not hold).
Reasons a continuous function may fail to be differentiable:
  - Corner or cusp: left and right derivative limits are not equal
  - Vertical tangent: the limit of the difference quotient is infinite (undefined slope)

POWER RULE
d/dx [x^r] = r * x^(r-1) for any real number r.

CONSTANT, SUM, DIFFERENCE, CONSTANT MULTIPLE RULES
d/dx [c] = 0
d/dx [f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x)
d/dx [f(x) - g(x)] = f'(x) - g'(x)
d/dx [c * f(x)] = c * f'(x)
Polynomials are differentiated by applying power rule term by term.

DERIVATIVES OF BASIC FUNCTIONS
d/dx [sin x] = cos x
d/dx [cos x] = -sin x
d/dx [e^x] = e^x
d/dx [ln x] = 1/x

PRODUCT RULE
d/dx [f(x) * g(x)] = f'(x) * g(x) + f(x) * g'(x)
Do NOT simply differentiate each factor and multiply - use the product rule.

QUOTIENT RULE
d/dx [f(x)/g(x)] = [f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)] / [g(x)]^2
Memory: "lo d-hi minus hi d-lo over lo squared"

DERIVATIVES OF OTHER TRIG FUNCTIONS (derived from sin, cos, product/quotient rules)
d/dx [tan x] = sec^2 x
d/dx [cot x] = -csc^2 x
d/dx [sec x] = sec x tan x
d/dx [csc x] = -csc x cot x
These follow from writing tan = sin/cos, etc., and applying the quotient rule.

USING A LIMIT AS A DEFINITION OF DERIVATIVE
If an expression is recognized as a limit definition of a derivative, it can be evaluated by identifying f and the point. For example, lim [sin(x+h) - sin(x)] / h as h -> 0 is the derivative of sin x, which is cos x.

KEY EXAM TIPS
- Show the product rule structure explicitly; do not just compute the final answer.
- Present difference quotients when approximating derivatives from tables.
- Use correct notation throughout; distinguish f'(x) from f(x).
- When differentiating at a specific value, show the procedure, then substitute.
