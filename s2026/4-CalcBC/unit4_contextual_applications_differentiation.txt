AP CALCULUS BC - UNIT 4: CONTEXTUAL APPLICATIONS OF DIFFERENTIATION
Exam Weighting: 6-9% BC
=================================================

INTERPRETING THE DERIVATIVE IN CONTEXT
The derivative f'(x) represents the instantaneous rate of change of f with respect to x.
Units: the unit of f'(x) = unit of f divided by unit of x.
Example: if f(t) is measured in gallons and t in minutes, f'(t) is in gallons per minute.

RECTILINEAR MOTION (STRAIGHT-LINE MOTION)
Position: s(t) or x(t)
Velocity: v(t) = s'(t) (rate of change of position)
Acceleration: a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t) (rate of change of velocity)
Speed: |v(t)| (always non-negative)
Particle moves right/forward when v(t) > 0; left/backward when v(t) < 0.
Particle speeds up when v(t) and a(t) have the same sign; slows down when opposite signs.
Particle changes direction when v(t) = 0 and v changes sign.

RATES OF CHANGE IN OTHER CONTEXTS
The same derivative structure applies to any changing quantity.
Be specific about which quantity is changing with respect to which variable.
Always use context-appropriate language (not "velocity" for non-motion problems).

RELATED RATES
When two or more quantities both depend on time, their rates of change are related.
Procedure:
  1. Identify all quantities and their rates.
  2. Write an equation relating the quantities (geometric, physical, etc.).
  3. Differentiate both sides with respect to t using the chain rule.
  4. Substitute known values and solve for the unknown rate.
  5. State answer with units and interpret in context.
Key: identify the independent variable that all quantities share (usually t).
Product rule or quotient rule may also be needed in addition to chain rule.

LOCAL LINEARITY AND LINEARIZATION
The tangent line at x = a is a local linear approximation of f near x = a.
Linearization: L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a)
If f is concave up at a, the tangent line is below the curve: L(x) underestimates f(x).
If f is concave down at a, the tangent line is above the curve: L(x) overestimates f(x).
Linearization is most accurate close to x = a.

L'HOSPITAL'S RULE
Used when a limit produces an indeterminate form 0/0 or infinity/infinity.
Prerequisite: verify that the limit produces one of these forms.
L'Hospital's Rule: if lim f(x) = 0 and lim g(x) = 0 (or both infinite), then:
  lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x)
provided the second limit exists.
Note: take derivatives of numerator and denominator separately (not the quotient rule).
Other indeterminate forms (0 * infinity, 1^infinity, etc.) are NOT assessed on the BC exam.

KEY EXAM TIPS
- Always verify that 0/0 or inf/inf is obtained before applying L'Hospital's Rule.
- For related rates: show the equation, differentiate it, then substitute.
- Units matter: include them in final answers.
- For linearization: state explicitly whether the approximation is an overestimate or underestimate, and justify using concavity.
- The conclusion of L'Hospital's Rule involves derivatives of numerator and denominator separately, not the derivative of their ratio.
