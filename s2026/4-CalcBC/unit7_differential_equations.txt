AP CALCULUS BC - UNIT 7: DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS
Exam Weighting: 6-9% BC
=================================================

WHAT IS A DIFFERENTIAL EQUATION
A differential equation is an equation relating a function and one or more of its derivatives.
Example: dy/dx = 2xy says the rate of change of y with respect to x equals 2 times the product of x and y.
There are infinitely many general solutions (differing by a constant of integration).
A particular solution is determined by an initial condition: a specific point (x_0, y_0) on the solution curve.

VERIFYING SOLUTIONS
To verify that a function is a solution to a differential equation:
  Differentiate the function, substitute into the DE, and confirm both sides are equal.

SLOPE FIELDS
A slope field is a graphical representation of a differential equation.
At each point (x, y) in the plane, draw a short line segment with slope equal to dy/dx at that point.
Slope fields show the qualitative behavior of all solution curves.
Different initial conditions lead to different particular solutions passing through the slope field.

EULER'S METHOD (BC only)
Euler's method approximates a solution to dy/dx = f(x, y) with initial condition y(x_0) = y_0.
Algorithm:
  x_{n+1} = x_n + h (step size)
  y_{n+1} = y_n + h * f(x_n, y_n)
Each step uses the slope (derivative) at the current point to estimate the next y-value.
Smaller step size gives better approximation.
Euler's method is a linear (tangent line) approximation applied repeatedly.

SEPARATION OF VARIABLES
Used when the DE can be written as g(y) dy = f(x) dx.
Procedure:
  1. Separate variables: move all y terms (including dy) to one side, all x terms (including dx) to the other.
  2. Integrate both sides.
  3. Add a constant of integration C on one side.
  4. Solve for y if possible (may involve e^C = another constant, typically renamed A or k).
  5. Apply initial condition to find the particular solution.
Failure to separate variables or omitting +C are common errors that cost points.

EXPONENTIAL GROWTH AND DECAY
Differential equation: dy/dt = k*y
This arises when "the rate of change of a quantity is proportional to the size of the quantity."
General solution: y = C * e^(kt) where C is the initial value y(0).
Or with initial condition y(0) = y_0: y = y_0 * e^(kt)
k > 0: exponential growth
k < 0: exponential decay

LOGISTIC GROWTH (BC only)
Differential equation: dy/dt = k * y * (1 - y/L)
Can also be modeled by dy/dt = k / L * y (L - y)
This arises when "rate of change is jointly proportional to the size of the quantity and the difference between the quantity and the carrying capacity."
Carrying capacity: as t -> infinity, y -> L.
The solution grows fastest when y = L/2 (halfway to carrying capacity), where d^2y/dt^2 = 0.
You do not need to solve this DE from scratch on the exam; recognize the form and use its properties.
Key logistic facts:
  - y = L/2 is the inflection point (fastest growth)
  - y approaches a as t -> infinity
  - if y < L, then dy/dt > 0 (y is increasing)
  - if y > L, then dy/dt < 0 (y is decreasing toward a)

GENERAL AND PARTICULAR SOLUTIONS
General solution: includes an arbitrary constant C; represents a family of solution curves.
Particular solution: the unique solution satisfying a given initial condition y(x_0) = y_0.
The function F(x) = y_0 + integral from x_0 to x of f(t) dt is a particular solution to dy/dx = f(x) satisfying F(x_0) = y_0.
Solutions may have domain restrictions depending on where the expressions are defined.

KEY EXAM TIPS
- Separate variables completely before integrating. Both sides must be cleanly separated.
- Always include +C after integrating. Using an initial condition to find C is often required.
- Show that your solution satisfies the DE by verifying (differentiating and substituting back).
- Identify differential equations in context: "rate proportional to quantity" means exponential; "rate proportional to quantity times (carrying capacity minus quantity)" means logistic.
- For slope fields: the slope at any point (x, y) is determined by substituting into dy/dx = f(x, y).
