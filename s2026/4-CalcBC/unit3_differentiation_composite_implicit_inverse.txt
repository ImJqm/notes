AP CALCULUS BC - UNIT 3: DIFFERENTIATION: COMPOSITE, IMPLICIT, AND INVERSE FUNCTIONS
Exam Weighting: 4-7% BC
=================================================

CHAIN RULE
For a composite function h(x) = f(g(x)):
  h'(x) = f'(g(x)) * g'(x)
In Leibniz notation: dy/dx = (dy/du) * (du/dx)
Reminder: always differentiate the "outer function" first, then multiply by the derivative of the "inner function."
Common errors:
  - Forgetting to multiply by the derivative of the inner function
  - Misidentifying the inner function

CHAIN RULE EXAMPLES
d/dx [sin(4x)] = cos(4x) * 4
d/dx [(x^2 + 1)^5] = 5(x^2 + 1)^4 * 2x
d/dx [e^(3x)] = e^(3x) * 3
d/dx [ln(5x)] = (1/(5x)) * 5 = 1/x

IMPLICIT DIFFERENTIATION
Used when y is defined implicitly by an equation in x and y rather than explicitly as y = f(x).
Differentiate both sides with respect to x.
Whenever you differentiate a term with y, apply the chain rule and multiply by dy/dx.
Then solve for dy/dx algebraically.
Example: for x^2 + y^2 = 25:
  2x + 2y(dy/dx) = 0 => dy/dx = -x/y

DIFFERENTIATING INVERSE FUNCTIONS
If f and f-inverse are inverse functions and f is differentiable at a point, then:
  (f^(-1))'(b) = 1 / f'(f^(-1)(b))
In words: the derivative of the inverse at b equals 1 divided by the derivative of f at the corresponding input.
Key: f(f^(-1)(b)) = b, and you must find f^(-1)(b) first (often by solving f(a) = b).

DERIVATIVES OF INVERSE TRIG FUNCTIONS
d/dx [arcsin x] = 1 / sqrt(1 - x^2)
d/dx [arccos x] = -1 / sqrt(1 - x^2)
d/dx [arctan x] = 1 / (1 + x^2)
d/dx [arccot x] = -1 / (1 + x^2)
d/dx [arcsec x] = 1 / (|x| * sqrt(x^2 - 1))
d/dx [arccsc x] = -1 / (|x| * sqrt(x^2 - 1))
With chain rule: d/dx [arctan(g(x))] = g'(x) / (1 + [g(x)]^2)

HIGHER-ORDER DERIVATIVES
The second derivative f''(x) is the derivative of f'(x).
The nth derivative f^(n)(x) is the derivative of f^(n-1)(x).
Notations: f''(x), d^2y/dx^2, y'', and for nth: f^(n)(x), d^n y/dx^n.
Second derivatives of implicitly defined functions involve y and dy/dx in the expression.

SELECTING PROCEDURES
Identify the form of the expression:
- Power function: power rule
- Product of two differentiable functions: product rule
- Quotient: quotient rule
- Composite function: chain rule
- Implicitly defined: implicit differentiation
- Inverse function: inverse function derivative formula

KEY EXAM TIPS
- Mastery of the chain rule is essential for all remaining units.
- For implicit differentiation, always apply chain rule to every y term.
- When differentiating products or quotients with chain rule inside, combine rules carefully.
- For inverse trig, memorize the six formulas; apply chain rule on top of them.
- Higher-order implicit derivatives: differentiate dy/dx again with respect to x using product/quotient/chain rules, and substitute known expressions for dy/dx.
