AP PHYSICS 1 - UNIT 6: ENERGY AND MOMENTUM OF ROTATING SYSTEMS
Exam Weighting: 10-15%

=============================================================
ROTATIONAL KINETIC ENERGY
=============================================================
Rotational kinetic energy of a rigid system:
  K_rot = (1/2) * I * omega^2

Rotational KE is a SCALAR and is always >= 0.

A rigid system can have rotational KE even while its center of mass is at rest
(because individual points on the system still have linear speed).

Total kinetic energy of a rolling/rotating+translating system:
  K_total = K_trans + K_rot
  K_total = (1/2) * m * v_cm^2 + (1/2) * I_cm * omega^2

For a point mass: K_rot = (1/2) * (mr^2) * omega^2 = (1/2) * m * v^2 = K_trans
  (rotational KE reduces to translational KE for a single point mass)

=============================================================
TORQUE AND WORK (ROTATIONAL)
=============================================================
A torque does work on a rigid system when the system rotates through an angular displacement.

Work done by a torque:
  W = tau * delta_theta
  (tau = torque in N*m; delta_theta = angular displacement in radians)

Work = area under the tau vs. theta (angular position) graph.

The rotational work-energy theorem: net work by all torques = change in rotational KE.

=============================================================
ANGULAR MOMENTUM AND ANGULAR IMPULSE
=============================================================
Angular momentum of a rigid system about a given axis:
  L = I * omega

Angular momentum of a point object (or object not clearly rotating about a fixed axis):
  L = m * v * r * sin(phi)
  where r = distance from reference point to object, phi = angle between r and v

Angular momentum is a vector; direction is along the axis of rotation (CW vs. CCW).
BOUNDARY: AP Physics 1 requires magnitude calculations only; direction is beyond scope.

The axis (reference point) chosen affects the value of angular momentum of an object.
Even an object moving in a straight line has angular momentum about a point not on its path.

Angular impulse:
  Angular impulse = tau_avg * delta_t

Angular impulse has the same direction as the torque.

Angular impulse = area under the tau vs. time graph.

Angular Impulse-Angular Momentum Theorem:
  tau_net * delta_t = delta_L = L_final - L_initial

Net torque = slope of the L vs. time graph.

Rotational form of Newton's Second Law (derived from angular impulse-momentum theorem):
  tau_net = I * alpha   (for constant rotational inertia)

=============================================================
CONSERVATION OF ANGULAR MOMENTUM
=============================================================
Angular momentum is conserved in ALL interactions.

If net external torque on the system = 0:
  L_initial = L_final   (angular momentum is constant)
  I_i * omega_i = I_f * omega_f

If net external torque is nonzero: angular momentum transfers between system and environment.
Change in angular momentum = angular impulse from external torques.

Newton's Third Law (rotational): angular impulse A exerts on B = equal and opposite to impulse B exerts on A.
Internal torques do NOT change the total angular momentum of a system.

Key consequence: if a non-rigid system changes shape (pulls mass closer or pushes it farther from axis),
angular velocity changes even with no external torque:
  I decreases => omega increases (spinning figure skater pulls arms in)
  I increases => omega decreases (figure skater extends arms out)

Total angular momentum of system = sum of all constituent angular momenta.

=============================================================
ROLLING (COMBINED TRANSLATION AND ROTATION)
=============================================================
Rolling without slipping (pure rolling):
  - Translational and rotational motions are directly linked.
  - For a sphere/cylinder of radius R: v_cm = R * omega; a_cm = R * alpha
  - The contact point has zero velocity relative to the surface.
  - Static friction at contact point does NO work (contact point doesn't move).
  - Total KE = (1/2) * m * v_cm^2 + (1/2) * I_cm * omega^2

Rolling while slipping:
  - Translational and rotational motions are NOT directly related.
  - Kinetic friction acts at the contact point; the point moves relative to surface.
  - Kinetic friction DISSIPATES energy.
  - Qualitatively: object eventually transitions from slipping to rolling without slipping
    (if friction acts, omega and v_cm adjust toward the rolling-without-slipping condition).

BOUNDARY: Precise math for rolling while slipping is beyond AP Physics 1.
Students must qualitatively explain linear and angular changes during slipping.

=============================================================
ORBITING SATELLITES
=============================================================
For a satellite orbiting a massive central body (central body mass >> satellite mass):
  - The motion of the central body is negligible.
  - Only gravitational force acts.

Gravitational PE between satellite and central body:
  U_g = -G * M * m / r
  (defined as zero when r = infinity; negative at all finite distances)

Circular orbit:
  - Constant mechanical energy E_mech = K + U_g
  - Constant gravitational PE (constant r)
  - Constant KE (constant speed)
  - Constant angular momentum

Elliptical orbit:
  - Constant total mechanical energy (E_mech = constant)
  - Constant angular momentum
  - PE and KE each change (faster and closer at nearest point; slower and farther at farthest)

Escape velocity: minimum speed to escape the gravitational pull (E_mech = 0):
  v_esc = sqrt(2 * G * M / r)
  (derived: K + U_g = 0  =>  (1/2)*m*v^2 - G*M*m/r = 0  =>  v = sqrt(2GM/r))

At escape velocity, satellite reaches zero speed at infinite distance.

=============================================================
KEY EQUATIONS SUMMARY
=============================================================
K_rot = (1/2) * I * omega^2
K_total = (1/2) * m * v^2 + (1/2) * I * omega^2
W_torque = tau * delta_theta
L = I * omega   (rigid body about fixed axis)
L = m * v * r * sin(phi)   (point mass)
Angular impulse = tau * delta_t = delta_L
tau_net = I * alpha   (constant I)
Rolling without slipping: v = R * omega; a = R * alpha
U_g = -G * M * m / r
v_esc = sqrt(2GM/r)

=============================================================
KEY REMINDERS
=============================================================
- Objects with the same mass and radius but different mass distributions roll down ramps at
  different speeds (disk beats hoop because disk has less rotational inertia).
- An ice skater spins faster by pulling arms in: I decreases, omega increases (L = I*omega is conserved).
- Rolling without slipping: static friction acts but does no work (energy conserved).
- Rolling with slipping: kinetic friction dissipates energy (mechanical energy decreases).
- Gravitational PE = -GMm/r is always negative; total E_mech of bound satellite is also negative.
- For circular orbit, increasing r means both slower speed and higher (less negative) total energy.
