AP PHYSICS 1 - UNIT 7: OSCILLATIONS
Exam Weighting: 5-8%

=============================================================
DEFINING SIMPLE HARMONIC MOTION (SHM)
=============================================================
SHM is a special case of periodic (repetitive) motion.

SHM occurs when a restoring force is proportional to displacement from equilibrium:
  F_net = -k * x   (for a spring; negative sign means force opposes displacement)

Restoring force: always directed TOWARD equilibrium (opposite to displacement).
Equilibrium position: where net force on the object is zero.

SHM occurs in:
  1. Mass on an ideal spring (spring force is linear restoring force).
  2. Simple pendulum at SMALL angles (restoring torque proportional to angular displacement).

For SHM: F_net is proportional to x (displacement from equilibrium) in magnitude,
and always points toward equilibrium.

=============================================================
FREQUENCY AND PERIOD OF SHM
=============================================================
Period T: time for one complete oscillation (seconds).
Frequency f: oscillations per second (Hz = 1/s).
  T = 1/f

Period of a mass-spring system (object of mass m on spring with constant k):
  T_s = 2 * pi * sqrt(m/k)

  - Increasing mass => longer period (slower oscillation)
  - Increasing spring constant k => shorter period (faster oscillation)
  - Period is INDEPENDENT of amplitude

Period of a simple pendulum (small angle approximation, angle < ~15 degrees):
  T_p = 2 * pi * sqrt(L/g)
  where L = length of the pendulum, g = 10 m/s^2

  - Increasing length L => longer period
  - Increasing g (stronger gravity) => shorter period
  - Period is INDEPENDENT of mass and amplitude (for small angles)
  - NOT valid for large angles

=============================================================
REPRESENTING AND ANALYZING SHM
=============================================================
At maximum displacement (amplitude A):
  - Displacement is maximum (+A or -A)
  - Velocity is ZERO
  - Acceleration is MAXIMUM (toward equilibrium)
  - Spring PE is maximum; KE is zero

At equilibrium (x = 0):
  - Displacement is zero
  - Velocity is MAXIMUM
  - Acceleration is ZERO
  - KE is maximum; spring PE is zero

The motion is sinusoidal: position, velocity, and acceleration all vary sinusoidally with time.
  x(t) = A * cos(omega*t) or A * sin(omega*t)
  (where omega = 2*pi*f = 2*pi/T = angular frequency in rad/s)

Position, velocity, and acceleration vs. time graphs for SHM:
  - Displacement graph is sinusoidal.
  - Velocity graph is shifted 90 degrees from displacement (velocity = 0 at extremes; max at center).
  - Acceleration graph is opposite to displacement (points toward equilibrium at all times).

AMPLITUDE has NO effect on period or frequency.
Changing amplitude changes the TOTAL energy and maximum speed.

=============================================================
ENERGY OF SIMPLE HARMONIC OSCILLATORS
=============================================================
Total mechanical energy of SHM system is constant (conserved):
  E_total = K + U = constant

For a mass-spring system:
  U_spring = (1/2) * k * x^2
  K = (1/2) * m * v^2
  E_total = (1/2) * k * A^2   (where A = amplitude)

Energy transformations during oscillation:
  At maximum displacement: E = all PE, K = 0
  At equilibrium: E = all KE, U = 0
  In between: E = K + U (both nonzero)

At equilibrium, maximum kinetic energy = maximum speed:
  (1/2) * m * v_max^2 = (1/2) * k * A^2
  v_max = A * sqrt(k/m)

Increasing amplitude INCREASES total energy and increases v_max and a_max.
Period and frequency are NOT affected by amplitude changes.

=============================================================
KEY EQUATIONS SUMMARY
=============================================================
T = 1/f
T_spring = 2 * pi * sqrt(m/k)
T_pendulum = 2 * pi * sqrt(L/g)   (small angles only)
F_restoring = -k * x   (spring)
E_total = K + U = (1/2)*k*A^2   (for spring-mass)
v_max = A * sqrt(k/m)

=============================================================
KEY REMINDERS
=============================================================
- Period of a pendulum depends ONLY on length and g; NOT on mass or amplitude (for small angles).
- Period of a spring-mass depends ONLY on mass and k; NOT on amplitude or g.
- Maximum speed occurs at equilibrium; zero speed at maximum displacement.
- Maximum acceleration occurs at maximum displacement; zero acceleration at equilibrium.
- Graphs of x, v, and a vs. time are all sinusoidal but phase-shifted relative to each other.
- Energy oscillates between KE and PE; total mechanical energy is constant.
- Doubling amplitude quadruples total energy (E proportional to A^2).
- The pendulum model as SHM is ONLY valid for small angles (roughly < 15 degrees).
